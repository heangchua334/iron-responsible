How far outside of the law should Heroes operate?

Iron Man is about a business man who turned into a superhero after experiencing a kidnapping by those who used weapons he developed in acts of violence. He creates a powered suit and becomes Iron Man, the titular hero who fights against terrorists that purchased weapons he developed through illegal means.

Synopsis

Iron Man

Tony Stark creates his Iron Man suit when held captive. He uses the suit to fight terrorists who gained access to weapons he developed. With his suit, he acts outside of United States jurisdiction as Iron Man.

Tony's design is stolen by Obediah Stane who creates his own suit. This leads to final battle where Tony, after being injured, has to use his prototype ark reactor which limits his power against Stane's Iron Monger.

Avengers: Age of Ultron

Tony Stark and Bruce Banner create an Artificial Intelligence with the goal of using it to protect the world. It's job would be to safeguard the world by analyzing threats.

Ultron, the A.I. developed by Stark and Banner determined that humans could not be stopped from going to war, and it's solution was ending human life. The question was then raised, should heroes operation without supervision?

Captain America: Civil War

The events of Avengers: Age of Ultron led to the massive destruction, and caused the government to push for a Hero Registration Act, which would limit a hero's ability to act without supervision.

This leads the discussion of whether Heroes should be held accountable, and who should be monitoring them. In addition, the issue with privacy and the rights of these heroes comes into question.

Reviews

Iron Man does an amazing job displaying the growth of a narcissistic businessman\weapons developer who realizes the weapons he created are being used for evil. He creates his suit of armor as a symbolism of a suit of armor to protect the people. The movie is not without faults, as it fails to describe the ramifications of his new powered suit and the increased scale of power that will be introduced into the world. In addition, it glosses over the acts of violence he inflicts on the terrorists, likely killing them while in foreign territory.

Despite getting low critical and fan reviews, I really enjoyed this movie. It shows that the best of intentions can have the worst of results. The noble goal of Tony Stark and Bruce Banner end of creating an entity that can cause the destruction of the Human Race. I felt this movie did a great job in showing the powers that a few heroes possess, and the horrors it can unlease despite their intentions.

This movie does a great job in showing the different views shared even in the modern world. Where violence by law authority has come under spotlight and the public demands proper police oversight. This movie shows that heroes operating outside of the law have become a threat to the common people. Tony Stark, after the failure of his Ultron Project in Avengers: Age of Ultron, now feel oversight is required. However such oversight and control means lack of rights for these other heroes.
